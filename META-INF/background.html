Abstract

The rapid development of technology brings about tremendous changes to our lives. In today’s high-tech world, internet has come to a huge resource pool for information gathering. In terms of copyright infringement, many problems can occur in the academic area and society. Therefore, new techniques is be developed to effectively solve this problem.

Background

In remote antiquity, the concept about intellectual property did not exist. Ideas were originally the common property for the literate elites. Articles were published by hand-copying. Scholars were able to make any digest or commentary on other works, which could consequently contain as much or as little initial materials as the author desired. During that time period, there was no standardized system of citation. Thousands of years ago, cheating was first prevalent on the Chinese civil service exams. Before the founding of the Modern Language Association and American Psychological Association at the end of 19th century, there was no any set rule describing properly cite quotations from others’ writings, which may have caused many cases of plagiarism nowadays. In the late 19th and early 20th centuries, cheating was widespread over all the college campuses. Therefore, it was no longer considered as academic dishonesty among students. “College fraternities” often operated so-called essay mills, where some kinds of term papers were kept and be resubmitted continuously by different students. Nevertheless, the name of the paper was modified. Plagiarism, as defined in the 1995 Random House Compact Unabridged Dictionary, is the “use or close imitation of the language and thoughts of another author and the representation of them as one’s own original work”. In academic, it is seen more broadly as the adoption or reproduction of original intellectual creation (such as concepts, ideas, methods, pieces of information or expression, etc.) of another author without due acknowledgement, in contexts that originality is acknowledged and rewarded.
